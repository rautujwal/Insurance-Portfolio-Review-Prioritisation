Insurance Portfolio Review Prioritisation (SQL, Python, Tableau)
Business Problem

Manual insurance portfolio review lacked a structured way to prioritise which segments and policies should be investigated first, causing review effort to be spread evenly instead of being focused on the areas driving the majority of claim workload.

Objective

Design a repeatable policy-level analysis workflow to identify which segments and policies generate disproportionate claim workload so review effort can be prioritised efficiently.

Dataset

Dataset sourced from a publicly available insurance portfolio dataset used for analytical workflow demonstration, containing over 1M policy records with over 20 attributes such as policy type, property type, location, insurance duration, premium amount, and claim counts.

Data Preparation

Enforced policy-level analytical grain (1 row = 1 policy)

Implemented validation checks for invalid premiums, claims, and duration

Removed duplicate policy IDs

Created derived analytical bands:

claim_band (0,1,2,3+)

tenure_band (0–1,2–3,4–5,6+)

Analytical Approach

Canonical policy base creation to enforce policy-level grain and validation flags.

Portfolio baseline KPIs to define normal performance (claims per policy, claims per $1k premium, claim-band distribution).

Segment benchmarking across Policy_Type × Location against portfolio baselines using lift and claim-share vs premium-share comparisons.

Contribution analysis ranking segments by claim share to identify drivers of review workload.

Workload concentration analysis measuring cumulative claim contribution across ranked segments (segment-level Pareto test).

Policy-level concentration analysis using deciles to test whether a subset of policies drives the majority of claims.

Tenure cohort comparison to evaluate whether claim workload concentrates in specific lifecycle stages.

Stability and dispersion checks to verify segment averages are reliable and not distorted by variability or sample size.

Key Findings

Segment benchmarking and dispersion checks showed structurally balanced claim behaviour across Policy_Type × Location segments, indicating no segment-level workload concentration.

Tenure analysis showed claim intensity remains nearly constant across tenure bands, with claim share closely tracking premium share.

Policy-level decile analysis revealed concentration: approximately 55% of total claims are generated by the top 30% of policies.

Outcome

The analysis reduces a large insurance portfolio into a prioritised set of policies for investigation, enabling review teams to focus effort where claim workload is most concentrated.

Tools Used

SQL • Python • Tableau
